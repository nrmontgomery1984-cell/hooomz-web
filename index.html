Pricing

Three tiers.
One visit.
You choose.

One site visit with LiDAR measurement delivers a formal three-tier quote — Good, Better, and Best. You pick what fits your budget and vision.

Good
Quality builder grade. Clean, durable, no-fuss.

Good tier uses products that meet or exceed code, selected based on manufacturer specs, installer familiarity, and industry track record. You'll end up with a result you're proud of — though in some areas, like flooring wear layer or paint sheen, there may be a practical trade-off to stay within budget. We spec Good carefully so those compromises don't land somewhere that matters for how you actually live. If you have pets, heavy traffic, or specific concerns, we'll flag that at quote time and make sure the spec accounts for it.

Better
Mid-range upgrades. Best value for most clients.

Better is where most clients land — and for good reason. The step up from Good removes most of the meaningful trade-offs: wider planks, better wear layers, higher-performing paint finishes. You're not paying for prestige at this tier, you're paying for the version that holds up where it counts — under pets, traffic, and real daily use. The Labs data backs this up: the durability gap between Good and Better is real in the categories that matter most. For the majority of main floor renovations in Southern New Brunswick, Better is the spec we'd choose for our own homes.

Best
Premium finishes. Designed to impress.

Best tier is for clients who want the room to feel finished in a way that's immediately noticeable. Premium hardwood, full gloss trim packages, crown moulding, custom millwork, feature walls, and specialty finishes where the scope calls for it. There are no compromises at this tier — products are specified for longevity, aesthetics, and performance. This is the version you show people when they ask who did your floors.

The Process

Six stages.
Every job.
No exceptions.

We run every renovation through SCRIPT — a six-stage production method ensuring nothing gets skipped and turnover is clean.

01
S
Shield
Surface protection placed, finishes documented, crew briefed. Nothing moves without substrate sign-off.
02
C
Clear
Demo and removal. Substrate issues flagged immediately — no surprises buried under new floors.
03
R
Ready
Layout confirmed, cut list generated, all materials staged and verified before installation begins.
04
I
Install
Daily progress logged, photos documented by room. Labs data captured during installation.
05
P
Punch
Full walkthrough, every open item tracked. Room-by-room sign-off. You're invited to join.
06
T
Turnover
Warranty docs delivered, care guide issued, Passport generated. Closed when all items resolved.
Hooomz Labs

We test
what we
recommend.

Most contractors go on manufacturer specs. We go further — Labs runs independent install and performance tests on every material we recommend, documenting moisture resistance, adhesion, acoustic performance, and long-term wear. Every spec we give you is backed by our own data.

Flooring Adhesion — LVP
Cortec Pro Plus outperformed three competing SKUs on direct-glue adhesion to concrete substrate. Recommended for all main floor applications.
Paint Adhesion — Trim Advance Series
Benjamin Moore Advance on primed MDF showed 3× better chip resistance than competitive alkyd alternatives at 30-day cure.
Wallpaper Install — Substrate Prep
Seam visibility and peel resistance on fresh drywall varies significantly by primer. Labs confirmed a two-coat oil-based primer approach as the current recommended standard before any wallpaper application.
